# Munich Re
As of December 1, 2024, Munich Re has reported robust financial performance for the year, marked by significant profitability and strategic resilience.

## Key Financial Highlights

- **Net Profit**: In the first half of 2024, Munich Re achieved a net profit of €3.76 billion, a substantial increase from the previous year. 

- **Third Quarter Performance**: The third quarter saw a net profit of €930 million, bringing the total net profit for the first nine months to €4.69 billion. 

- **Return on Equity (RoE)**: The company reported a RoE of 24.3% for the first half of 2024, reflecting strong profitability. 

- **Insurance Revenue**: Insurance revenue from contracts issued increased to €45.51 billion in the first three quarters, up from €42.91 billion in the same period the previous year. 

## Segment Performance

- **Reinsurance**: The reinsurance segment contributed a net result of €3.23 billion in the first half, with a RoE of 24.9%. 

- **ERGO (Primary Insurance)**: ERGO reported a net result of €535 million in the same period, achieving a RoE of 21.3%. 

## Outlook

Munich Re maintains its annual net profit target of €5.0 billion for the 2024 financial year. The strong performance in the first three quarters increases the likelihood of meeting or surpassing this goal. 

## Strategic Developments

The company has benefited from high reinsurance prices, which are expected to remain stable through the end of the year. This pricing environment has contributed to record profits and reflects Munich Re's strategic positioning in the market. 

## Conclusion

Munich Re's financial results for 2024 demonstrate its robust operational performance and strategic resilience. The company is well-positioned to achieve its financial targets and continue delivering value to shareholders.
